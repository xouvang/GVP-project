General Vang Pao

A General, Leader, Hero

Timeline

  • December 8, 1929 - Born in a Hmong village named Nonghet. This is located in Central Xiangkhuang Provice, in the northeastern region of Laos, where his father, Neng Chu Vang, was a county leader.
  • 1942 - Was a courier for the Hmong leader, prince in Laos, and the French
  • 1950 - Became a part of the army
  • 1952 - Pao was the only Hmong who attended the French Police Academy. He was ranked 7 out of 56 students.
  • Date Unknown - During WWII, Vang became a translator for the Japanese in Laos, and trained in Vietnam at a school.
  • 1961 - Recruited by the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) to lead a secrety army of Hmong soldiers to fight against the Laotian Communists and their North Vietnamese counterparts.
  • 1975 - The Secret War finally ends. Vang and his fellow Hmong community moved from the mountains to the U.S., and lived in Montana.
  • 1977 - Established an agency to help the Hmong society, called Lao family Community Organizations
  • Early 1980's - Vang and a group of his allies formed Neo Hom to help those who remains in Laos to continue the fight to overthrow the Communists.
  • 1986 - Moves and settles in California.
  • 2003 - He meets and negotiates with the Vietnamese government officials about keeping a trading relationship with Laos and having the Vietnamese to keep the Hmong community safe by pressuring the Laotians to stop provoking them.
  • 2007 - Vang Pao and eleven others were being charged with trying to overthrow the Laotian Government. Many have protested for Vang to be released.
  • October 2007 - Vang's case was dismissed in Sacramento. Approximately 10,000 Hmong people went and testifed of his innocence.
  • January 6, 2011 - General Vang Pao passed away from pneumonia at Clovis Community Medical Center, at the age of 84.
